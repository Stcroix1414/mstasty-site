The Setup Is Actually Cooked

Live stats, a custom console, a Discord bot, and now a one-click blog publisher. This got out of hand in the best way.

When I said I wanted to stream, I did not mean I wanted to build a whole piece of infrastructure around it. And yet here we are.

TastyBot started as a chat bot. Just something to respond to commands and track deaths because manually typing !death every time I got clapped was beneath me. That was supposed to be it. Then I needed a web console to control it. Then the console needed a death counter. Then the death counter needed an OBS overlay. Then the overlay needed a Stream Deck button. One thing kept compounding and I never said stop.

What's Actually Running Now

The bot runs 24/7 on a Linux server. It handles chat commands, tracks LP and deaths, manages predictions, logs every session. There's a web console at a local IP where I can control everything mid-stream without touching chat — set rank, log placements, adjust the death counter, send messages, end the stream and save the session. The whole thing.

The site you're on right now pulls live data from that bot through a Cloudflare Tunnel. When I'm streaming, those stats on the homepage are real. If it says 14 deaths, those are real deaths. I earned every single one.

The site also has a Discord bot that posts go-live alerts, welcomes new members, and lets me manage the death counter from Discord slash commands. Yes, I built that too.

The Blog Thing

As of today the console has a Blog tab. I fill in the title, content blocks, hit Publish — and it writes the HTML, updates the post index, commits to GitHub, and pushes. The site updates in about a minute. I'm writing this post to test it, and also because I think it's genuinely impressive and I wanted to talk about it.

No CMS. No WordPress. No third-party anything. Just a bot that knows how to run git commands.

What's Next

Streams are weekends at 3PM PST. TFT ranked, Windrose, League, and whatever else. Also building two reef tanks in the coming months — a 1.5 gallon pico and a 17 gallon cube. That'll probably end up on this blog too because why not, this is my website and I do what I want.

Come hang. Discord link is everywhere on this site. TastyBot will greet you personally.
